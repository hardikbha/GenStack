в—Ҹ GenStack Architecture вҖ” End-to-End Walkthrough

  This is a detailed walkthrough of the diagram, in the order data actually
  flows through the system. I'll cover every box, every label, every arrow.

  ---
  0. Input вҖ” single face image

  A single RGB face image enters from the left edge of the diagram (the woman's
  portrait). This same image is fanned out into two parallel branches (PACT and
  Prism-SFT) and is the only raw input вҖ” everything downstream is computed from
  it.

  The two branches process the image independently and in parallel. Their
  outputs are then fused by the third stage (MoE + gate) on the right.

  ---
  1. Branch 1 вҖ” PACT (Discriminative path) вҖ” top half, blue dashed box

  This branch produces a single scalar fake probability by treating the image as
   an ordinary CLIP feature map and adding a small forensic head on top.

  1a. Vision Encoder вҖ” CLIP ViT-L/14 (yellow trapezoid with snowflake)

  - The trapezoid is the CLIP ViT-Large/14 vision encoder.
  - The snowflake icon = frozen. Not a single weight in this 304M-parameter
  encoder is trained вҖ” it serves as a fixed, off-the-shelf semantic feature
  extractor.
  - Why CLIP: pretrained on 400M image-text pairs, it gives a strong
  semantic-level embedding. Things like "this is a face", "this looks
  studio-lit", "this looks anime-stylized" вҖ” all of that is already in the
  features.
  - Output: a sequence of patch tokens (a 16Г—16 grid вҶ’ 256 patch tokens for a
  224Г—224 image, plus a CLS token). The CLS token re-appears later as input to
  the gate (right side).

  1b. PGAD вҖ” Prototype-Guided Artifact Detector  (light-green box)

  - 128 prototypes Г— 6 banks. The 6 banks are labeled: Texture, Edges, Color, 
  Geometry, Semantics, Frequency.
  - Each "prototype" is a learnable reference vector that represents one
  canonical pattern of a forgery cue. Example: a "high-frequency checkerboard"
  prototype in the Frequency bank, or a "skin-pore over-smoothing" prototype in
  the Texture bank.
  - Operation: PGAD does a cross-attention between CLIP patch tokens and the 768
   (= 128 Г—В 6) prototypes. For each image patch, "which forensic prototype does
  this most resemble, and how strongly?"
  - Why 6 banks: deepfake artifacts come in distinct flavors вҖ” GANs leave
  frequency-domain fingerprints; diffusion leaves smooth texture; face-swap
  leaves geometric warps at the boundary. Splitting by bank lets the system
  answer cue-by-cue rather than forcing one monolithic detector.
  - Output: a multi-channel heatmap-style representation that condenses where
  and what kind of artifact is present.

  1c. Classification Head (peach box with a small graph icon)

  - A shallow 2-layer MLP with a learnable temperature.
  - Takes the pooled PGAD output and produces a single sigmoid score.

  1d. Continuous Fake Probability  $p_v(x) \in [0,1]$ (light-green box)

  - Final scalar from PACT. 0 = real, 1 = fake, continuous in between (so you
  keep calibration information, not just a hard label).
  - This single number is one half of the (p_v, b_p) pair that gets fused later.
  - Standalone, this branch already reaches 84.95% on HydraFake.

  Big picture for Branch 1: it's a fast, frozen-backbone, semantically-grounded
  discriminator. It's strong on most generators but plateaus when the forgery is
   semantic (e.g. ICLight relighting), because semantic features are exactly
  what CLIP is calibrated for вҖ” relit fakes look semantically ordinary.

  ---
  2. Branch 2 вҖ” Prism-SFT (Multi-view MLLM reasoner) вҖ” bottom half, red dashed 
  box

  This branch produces a binary verdict plus a structured natural-language 
  rationale, by giving an MLLM three different "views" of the same image.

  2a. Three input views вҖ” the three small images on the left of this branch

  The same input image is rendered three ways:

  - Original RGB image (top вҖ” comes directly from the input arrow).
  - FFT Map (the rainbow-colored spectrum image): take the 2-D Fourier transform
   of the image, take log-magnitude, render with a JET colormap. This makes
  high-frequency artifacts visible вҖ” GAN-generated images often have a tell-tale
   cross or spike pattern in the spectrum.
  - Noise Residual Map (the gray ghostly image): compute  $x -
  \text{GaussianBlur}_{\sigma=3}(x)$. This subtracts a smoothed version of the
  image, leaving only the high-frequency residual вҖ” i.e., the camera/sensor
  noise pattern (or the absence of one, in synthetic images). Real cameras leave
   a coherent noise floor; generators often leave none, or a different one.

  These three views give the MLLM three orthogonal channels of evidence.
  Crucially, FFT and noise-residual are not in the channel a normal vision
  encoder reads вҖ” handing them in explicitly is what lets the MLLM "see"
  forensic cues it would otherwise be blind to.

  2b. Multi-image Prompt (light-blue box)

  - Literal text shown:
  [image][image][image]
  Determine the authenticity of this face using the original image and forensic
  maps.
  - The [image] tokens are placeholders that the MLLM tokenizer replaces with
  the three images' visual embeddings, in order: RGB, FFT, noise-residual.
  - The natural-language instruction tells the model what to do with them.

  2c. InternVL3-8B Backbone (red box with flame icon)

  - InternVL3-8B: a vision-language model with an 8B-parameter LLM and a vision
  encoder that natively handles multi-image input.
  - The flame icon = trainable. Concretely we apply LoRA adapters with rank
  r=128, Оұ=256, and we unfreeze the vision encoder so it can adapt to the
  unusual statistics of FFT spectra and noise residuals (which are far outside
  the distribution of typical pretraining images).
  - Trained via SFT on the prism_sft_36k dataset using AdamW, lr 5Г—10вҒ»вҒө, 3
  epochs, 4 GPUs.

  2d. LLM Rationale (Structured) вҖ” gray box bottom-right of this branch

  The model is forced to output a fixed XML-tagged trace:
  <plan> ... </plan>            вҶҗ which regions/views to inspect
  <examine region="..."> ... </examine>   вҶҗ cite spectral/residual evidence
  <conclude> ... </conclude>    вҶҗ synthesize the evidence
  <answer> real / fake </answer>
  - The structure is enforced by the SFT training data вҖ” the model never
  generates free-form prose, it always emits these four blocks in order.
  - Why structured: (i) forces the model to actually inspect before deciding,
  (ii) gives us an interpretable trace to show users, (iii) the final <answer>
  is trivial to parse.

  2e. Binary Reasoning Verdict  $b_p(x) \in {0,1}$ (purple box)

  - Parsed from the <answer> tag вҖ” 1 if "fake", 0 if "real".
  - This is the second half of the (p_v, b_p) pair.
  - Standalone, Prism-SFT alone reaches 88.22% on HydraFake.

  Big picture for Branch 2: it's a slow, multi-view, reasoning-based detector.
  Its FFT and noise channels catch generators where CLIP semantics fail
  (frequency-domain GAN signatures, etc.), and its structured rationale is what
  makes the system explainable.

  ---
  3. MoE + Gating вҖ” fusion stage вҖ” right side, orange box

  Here the two scalars  $p_v, b_p$ are combined into the final verdict. The 
  whole point of this stage: the correct way to combine the two branches is
  generator-dependent вҖ” sometimes PACT is right, sometimes Prism is right,
  sometimes their disagreement is what carries the signal.

  3a. Joint Branch O/P  $(p_v(x), b_p(x))$  (yellow box)

  - Just packs the two branch outputs into a 2-d feature  $(p_v, b_p)$.
  - This is what the experts consume.

  3b. Learned Soft Gate  $\pi(x)$  (yellow box)

  - $\pi(x) = (\pi_1, \dots, \pi_{23})$  is a 23-dimensional probability vector
  over HydraFake's 23 generators.
  - Implemented as a multinomial logistic regression on the в„“вӮӮ-normalized CLIP 
  CLS embedding of the image (shown by the dashed arrow on the right side: the
  CLS feature was already extracted by Branch 1, so the gate is essentially
  free).
  - "Soft" = doesn't pick one generator and forward only there; it produces a
  distribution (probabilities summing toВ 1) and uses it as mixing weights.
  - Diagnostic: the gate's top-1 generator-id accuracy is 71.0%, top-3 is 81.3%
  (vs 4.35% chance for 23-way classification). It's not perfect, but it's
  accurate enough that the soft mixing recovers from the residual error.

  3c. Per-Generator Mixture of Experts  (large green box with  $f_1, f_2, f_3, 
  \dots, f_{23}$)

  - 23 small Gradient-Boosting expert classifiers, one per HydraFake generator.
  - Each expert  $f_g \colon \mathbb{R}^2 \to [0,1]$  is fit on the (p_v, b_p)
  pairs from images of generator g (and a corresponding sample of real images),
  and learns the best decision rule for that generator.
    - For most generators that rule is roughly "average of p_v and b_p".
    - For ICLight, the rule is non-monotone in p_v вҖ” neither base detector alone
   exceeds 56%, but their disagreement is highly informative, and a
  Gradient-Boosted tree captures that interaction effortlessly. A single global
  combiner cannot.

  3d. Final Prediction  (orange box)

  $$
  \hat{y}(x) ;=; \sum_{g \in G}, \pi_g(x),\cdot,f_g!\bigl(p_v(x),,b_p(x)\bigr)
  $$

  - Soft mixture: every expert is consulted; their answers are weighted by the
  gate's confidence in each generator.
  - This is exactly an MoE forward pass вҖ” but with experts pre-trained
  per-generator and a logistic-regression gate, no end-to-end joint training of
  experts and gate.
  - Cost: the entire fusion stage is <100K parameters, <0.1 ms/image, <1 minute 
  to train from cached base predictions.

  3e. Verdict вҖ” Real or Fake  (blue box)

  Threshold  $\hat{y}(x)$  at 0.5 to get the final binary label.

  ---
  4. End-to-end data flow вҖ” the one-paragraph version

  An image enters from the left. It is sent to Branch 1 (PACT): a frozen CLIP
  ViT-L/14 encodes it; PGAD (128 prototypes Г— 6 forensic banks) produces a
  multi-bank artifact map; a small classification head emits a continuous fake
  probability  $p_v$. In parallel, the image is sent to Branch 2 (Prism-SFT):
  the system computes its FFT spectrum and its high-pass noise residual, packs
  all three views (RGB, FFT, noise) into a multi-image prompt, and feeds them to
   a LoRA-fine-tuned InternVL3-8B. The MLLM produces a structured rationale
  (<plan>вҖҰ<examine>вҖҰ<conclude>вҖҰ<answer>) from which we parse a binary verdict
  $b_p$. The pair  $(p_v, b_p)$ is consumed by the fusion stage: a learned soft
  gate  $\pi(x)$  вҖ” built on CLIP's CLS embedding (already free from Branch 1) вҖ”
   outputs a 23-way distribution over generators, and 23 per-generator 
  Gradient-Boosting experts each emit a fake-probability for the input. Their
  answers are mixed by the gate to produce  $\hat{y}(x) = \sum_g
  \pi_g(x),f_g(p_v, b_p)$, thresholded at 0.5 to give the final Real / Fake
  verdict.

  6. Anticipated questions during the talk

  - "Why two branches and not one bigger model?" вҖ” They fail on disjoint subsets
   of inputs (CLIP misses frequency artifacts; the MLLM, on a single RGB view,
  misses things CLIP catches). Structurally complementary errors compress when
  fused.
  - "Why per-generator experts and not one combiner?" вҖ” On generators like
  ICLight, the decision boundary is non-monotone in (p_v, b_p) вҖ” disagreement is
   the signal. A single global classifier cannot express this; per-generator GB
  can.
  - "Doesn't per-generator routing require knowing the generator at inference?"
  вҖ” No. The gate predicts the generator from the image (top-1 71%, top-3 81%).
  Soft mixing absorbs the residual gate error.
  - "Where do the 8 Forgery Prompt Tokens you mention in the paper fit?" вҖ” They
  sit between the patch-tokenizer and the frozen CLIP encoder in the PACT branch
   (not drawn as a separate box in this diagram вҖ” they're inside the "Vision
  Encoder" trapezoid). They're 8 learnable tokens prepended to the patch
  sequence; together with PGAD they're the only trainable parameters in PACT
  (~1.24 M).
  - "Can the gate handle a brand-new generator?" вҖ” Yes, with a small drop. The
  Leave-One-Generator-Out study shows the soft mix degrades by only вҲ’2.88 pp on
  average when the held-out generator is unseen.

  That's the whole pipeline, end to end, every box, every arrow.
